# Marketing Board Meeting — Atlas Sovereign SMB coworker kickoff (2026-07-24)

**Experiment:** `experiments/002-marketing_team_workflow/`

**Pipeline:** marketing-client

## Proposal

Approve an SMB-primary kickoff for Atlas Sovereign’s Agentic AI Division that locks “trustworthy digital coworker” (augmentation-first, claim-safe) positioning, LinkedIn + founder-led + waitlist as the channel bet, and qualified waitlist→discovery as North Star—with ASSUMPTION $0–500 media and a 6-week working timeline—contingent on client confirming budget band, dates, and claim review before package pricing or any outbound.

## Roster & Roles

- growth-marketer
- content-strategist
- sales-lead
- revops-analyst
- skeptic — **DEVIL'S ADVOCATE** (must argue strongest case AGAINST the proposal)

## Round 0 — Independent Positions

*(Agents blind to each other. Orchestrator appends each response verbatim.)*

### Member A (growth-marketer)

- **RECOMMENDATION:** adopt-with-changes
- **TOP 3 RISKS / objections:**
  1. Near-zero media ($0–500) makes CAC almost entirely founder-LinkedIn-dependent; weak posting cadence or thin founder authority kills top-of-funnel volume.
  2. 40–60 waitlist → 12–18 discovery in 14 days is aggressive for a cold SMB organic LinkedIn + waitlist launch in 6 weeks with paid deferred.
  3. Waitlist without paid or a live site can inflate vanity signups; without a hard “qualified” definition, CAC looks cheap while discovery conversion collapses.
- **KEY ASSUMPTION:** Founder can sustain a claim-safe LinkedIn cadence (≈3–5 posts/week + reply time) that generates enough ICP-fit traffic for the waitlist→discovery North Star without paid.
- **CONFIDENCE:** 4
- **CHANGES:** Stage funnel targets (soft ramp weeks 1–3, full North Star weeks 4–6); make founder cadence + “qualified waitlist” criteria go-conditions alongside budget/dates/claim review.

### Member B (content-strategist)

- **RECOMMENDATION:** adopt-with-changes
- **TOP 3 RISKS / objections:**
  1. Charter language (“autonomous… digital employees”) will leak into founder posts and SEO unless a hard claim lexicon + review gate is locked before any public copy.
  2. LinkedIn + waitlist with $0–500 media can win trust conversations but starves organic discovery—no thin content spine (1 cornerstone page + 3–4 pillar posts) means weak search/share permanence after the 6-week burst.
  3. SMB-primary positioning may flatten nonprofit/freelance proof points; without segment-aware examples, “fit your stack” stays abstract and brand voice sounds generic vs. category peers.
- **KEY ASSUMPTION:** Client will approve a claim-safe lexicon that permanently markets “trustworthy digital coworker / augmentation-first” (Agentic AI Division only) and will not insist on “autonomous digital employees” in outbound copy.
- **CONFIDENCE:** 4
- **CHANGES:** (1) Mandate claim rules + banned/allowed phrases in the messaging house before package pricing or outbound. (2) Keep SMB-primary but add 1–2 secondary-segment proof lines (nonprofit/freelance). (3) Include a minimal SEO/content spine in the plan.

### Member C (sales-lead)

- **RECOMMENDATION:** adopt-with-changes
- **TOP 3 RISKS / objections:**
  1. Discovery conversion is the bottleneck, not waitlist volume — 12–18 discovery / 4–6 SQL in 6 weeks assumes founder calendar capacity, response SLA, and show-rate; none are locked.
  2. Qualification gate is underspecified — without explicit disqualify rules, 3-touch nurture will book tire-kickers and burn scarce discovery slots.
  3. Handoff SLA from qualified waitlist → booked discovery is missing — no owner, no time-to-first-touch, no no-show recovery.
- **KEY ASSUMPTION:** Founder (or designated closer) can run ~3–4 discovery calls/week with ≤48h booking SLA and claim-safe talk tracks.
- **CONFIDENCE:** 3
- **CHANGES:** Lock ICP qualification checklist + hard disqualifiers; define discovery SLA; treat 40–60 / 12–18 / 4–6 as capacity-contingent assumptions, not commit numbers, until founder calendar and budget band are confirmed.

### Member D (revops-analyst)

- **RECOMMENDATION:** adopt-with-changes
- **TOP 3 RISKS / objections:**
  1. “Qualified waitlist” is not an operational stage yet — without explicit field/score rules, the North Star will mix vanity signups with true MQLs.
  2. Dual tracking (HubSpot optional + lightweight fallback) breaks lifecycle integrity unless one SOR is locked before launch.
  3. North Star depends on a bookable discovery path and ownership SLA — if calendar/routing isn’t defined, “books discovery within 14 days” cannot be measured.
- **KEY ASSUMPTION:** Client will confirm measurement SOR (HubSpot vs approved fallback), field contract, and scoring definitions for MQL/SQL before package pricing or any outbound.
- **CONFIDENCE:** 4
- **CHANGES:** Freeze scoring contract (Lead / MQL / SQL / Opportunity); choose one measurement SOR; require booking instrument as package prerequisite.

### Member E (skeptic) — DEVIL'S ADVOCATE

- **RECOMMENDATION:** reject
- **TOP 3 RISKS / objections:**
  1. Approving a locked strategy while budget, dates, and claim review remain ASSUMPTIONS is rubber-stamp theater—contingencies admit the plan is not decision-ready.
  2. LinkedIn + founder-led + waitlist at $0–500 media in 6 weeks for custom agentic-AI sales is a demand fantasy; design package cannot execute founder posting + hosting the waitlist.
  3. Claim-safe coworker framing still concentrates legal/reputational risk; locking SMB-primary abandons nonprofit/freelance without evidence; multi-division noise will dilute thin kickoff signal.
- **KEY ASSUMPTION:** That an unestablished founder can convert organic LinkedIn + a waitlist into enough *qualified* discovery conversations in ~6 weeks with near-zero media.
- **CONFIDENCE:** 4
