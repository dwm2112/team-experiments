## Section B — Fill-in Guide

### Placeholders

| Placeholder | What to put | How to pick the strongest answer |
|---|---|---|
| `[VAGUE_ASK_STORY]` | A time you took a loose problem ("we need X somehow") and turned it into a shipped feature or tool | Prefer stories where *you* defined requirements. Name the domain, the decision you made, and the concrete deliverable. Avoid "I built a dashboard" with no before/after. |
| `[AUTOMATION_STORY]` | A scraper, reporting pipeline, workflow, or scheduled job that replaced manual work | Quantify if you can (hours saved, error rate, frequency). "Automated weekly recon that used to take 4 hours" beats "built a Python script." |
| `[DATA_INTEGRATION_STORY]` | Moving, reconciling, or presenting data — ideally Stripe, QuickBooks, or a similar third-party API | Lead with the business outcome (books matched payments, invoices synced, reports trustworthy). Mention reading docs and shipping without a hand-held walkthrough. |
| `[HONESTY_LINE]` | One thing you haven't done (or haven't done recently) that might come up, plus your approach | Match their language: "I haven't shipped a QuickBooks Online integration yet, but I've done Stripe + similar REST accounting APIs — I'd start with their sandbox, map the chart of accounts, and verify with a reconciliation report before going live." Do **not** invent gaps; pick a real one. |
| `[QUESTION_1]` | A business-context question | Shows you ask *why* before *how*. Examples below. |
| `[QUESTION_2]` | A stack / data / integration question | Shows you care about their existing system (JS/HTML5, likely PHP/AngularJS legacy). Examples below. |
| `[CITY_OR_STATE]` | US location | Required — they filter non-US. Be specific enough to be credible ("Austin, TX" or "remote, US — EST"). |
| `[HOURS_PER_WEEK]` | Number under 30 | Match their cap. e.g. 15–25. Don't oversell availability you can't keep for 6+ months. |
| `[RATE]` | Your hourly rate | Stay inside $70–90 unless you have a clear reason to negotiate later. |
| `[YOUR_NAME]` | Name as on Upwork | Keep consistent with your profile. |

### Choosing stories — priority order

1. **If you have Stripe or QuickBooks experience** → put it in `[DATA_INTEGRATION_STORY]`. That is their clearest technical signal.
2. **If you have PHP and/or AngularJS (or Angular 1.x)** → weave one sentence into story #1 or #3, or add a short bridge line after the stories: *"I've also extended PHP + AngularJS codebases, so I'm comfortable working inside an existing stack rather than pushing a rewrite."* This is a differentiator most applicants will miss.
3. **If you lack one of the three story types** → use two strong stories and drop the weakest slot rather than inventing fluff. Two specific stories beat three vague ones.
4. **Prefer recent, measurable, and business-facing** over pure tech demos.

### Suggested clarifying questions (pick 2)

Business-context options for `[QUESTION_1]`:
- What's the main workflow this hire would unblock first — billing reconciliation, reporting, or something else entirely?
- When a "vague ask" lands on your plate, what does a good first deliverable look like for you (a working prototype, a data audit, a shipped feature)?
- Where does most of the pain live today — getting data *into* the system, keeping it consistent, or presenting it to the team?

Stack / data options for `[QUESTION_2]`:
- Is the current app primarily JS on a PHP/AngularJS-style stack, or has it already moved toward a newer frontend?
- Are Stripe and/or QuickBooks already in production, or is integrating one of them part of the near-term work?
- How much of the day-to-day is SQL/reconciliation vs. new UI — so I know where to lean first?

### AI-tooling line

The template already includes a philosophy line. Only customize if you use different tools by name — keep the *explore / verify / own the review* framing. Do not list five AI products.

## Section D — Do / Don't

### Do
- Mirror their language: vague ask, without hand-holding, reconcile, push back, honest about gaps.
- Lead with behavior and outcomes, not a skills laundry list.
- Keep it skimmable: short paragraphs, numbered stories, clear CTA.
- Confirm **US-based** explicitly — this is a hard filter.
- Ask 1–2 sharp questions (demonstrates the collaborator trait *in the proposal itself*).
- Include one real honesty line — it builds trust and screens you as not a vibe-coder.
- Sound like a person who writes for busy clients: concrete, calm, specific.

### Don't
- Open with "I am a passionate full-stack developer with X years of experience…"
- Paste a resume or laundry list of frameworks.
- Name-drop Claude/Codex without a judgment philosophy (they explicitly hate paste-until-it-runs).
- Claim Stripe/QuickBooks/PHP/AngularJS you don't have.
- Overpromise availability (>30 hrs/wk) or rewrite their stack.
- Use buzzwords: synergy, leverage, cutting-edge, rockstar, 10x.
- Write in obvious AI tone: "I'd be thrilled to embark on this exciting journey," stacked emojis, or perfectly symmetrical three-beat marketing cadence.
- Send from outside the U.S., or be vague about location — you will be filtered out.

### US-location gate (non-negotiable)

Only apply if you can honestly say you are located in the United States. Put city/state or "Remote, US — [timezone]" in the logistics line. If you are not US-based, do not submit this proposal to this posting.
